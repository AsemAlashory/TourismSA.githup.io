Tourism in Saudi Arabia is a growing sector that offers a variety of attractions and experiences for visitors. Saudi Arabia is home to the two holiest cities in Islam, Mecca and Medina, where millions of pilgrims perform their religious duties every year. Saudi Arabia also has a rich cultural heritage, diverse natural landscapes, and modern amenities that cater to different tastes and preferences. Some of the popular tourist destinations in Saudi Arabia are: Medina: The second holiest city in Islam and the final resting place of Prophet Muhammad. Medina is a sacred destination for Muslims who visit the Prophet’s Mosque, one of the largest and oldest mosques in the world. Medina also has historical and cultural sites, such as the Battle of Uhud, the Al-Baqi’ cemetery, and the Seven Mosques. Jeddah: A coastal city on the Red Sea, known for its sculptures, fountains, coral houses, and beach resorts. Jeddah is a cosmopolitan and vibrant city that offers shopping, dining, entertainment, and cultural activities. Jeddah also has the Al-Rahma Mosque, a Venetian-style mosque on the sea, and the King Fahd’s Fountain, the tallest fountain in the world. Riyadh: The capital and largest city of Saudi Arabia, and a hub of business, politics, and culture. Riyadh has a mix of modern and traditional architecture, such as the Kingdom Centre, the Al Faisaliyah Centre, and the Masmak Fortress. Riyadh also has museums, parks, gardens, and malls that showcase the history, art, and lifestyle of Saudi Arabia. Ta’if: A mountainous city in the Hejaz region, known for its pleasant climate, rose farms, and fruit orchards. Ta’if is a popular summer destination for locals and tourists who enjoy the scenic views, the fresh air, and the fragrant roses. Ta’if also has historical and religious sites, such as the Shubra Palace, the Wadi Mitna, and the Al-Idrisi Mosque. Abha: A city in the south-west of Saudi Arabia, surrounded by the Sarawat Mountains and the Asir National Park. Abha is famous for its greenery, waterfalls, lakes, and forests. Abha also has a unique cultural identity, reflected in its architecture, art, and cuisine. Abha also has attractions such as the Abha Palace Hotel, the Abha Dam, and the Cable Car. These are just some of the many places you can visit in Saudi Arabia. You can also explore other cities, such as Khobar, Dammam, Jubail, Tabuk, Diriyah, Yanbu, Buraydah, Hofuf, and Makkah
